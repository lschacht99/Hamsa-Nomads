The origin

Traveling far while trying to stay rooted.

Hamsa Nomads began with a simple truth we lived again and again: exploring the world while keeping our faith is a treasure.

Traveling as observant Jews is not always easy. You think about food, Shabbat, holidays, community, vessels, timing, and small details most travelers never notice. But those same challenges become part of the beauty.

Along the way, we faced unexpected meetings — the kind you cannot plan and cannot force. A few Hebrew letters in a faraway street. A stranger who remembers a Jewish connection. A local Jewish community opening its doors. A Shabbat meal appearing when we thought we would be alone.

Those encounters changed the way we look at life. They taught us that Jewish identity is not only something we carry privately; it is something that connects us to people, places, histories, and stories across the world.

We traveled through India, Sri Lanka, Vietnam, Japan, South Korea, Morocco, Israel, France, and the United States — each place shaping us differently. Some moments were practical, like figuring out how to keep kosher in unfamiliar conditions. Some were spiritual, like toveling vessels in the waves of Sri Lanka. Some were human, like finding Jewish warmth in places where we expected to find none.

Hamsa Nomads was born from those moments: the challenge, the wonder, the improvisation, and the feeling that Jewish life can travel with you — not as a burden, but as a source of depth.

Every trip became more than travel. It became a way to meet people, discover hidden Jewish stories, and understand what it means to stay rooted while moving through the world.
Journeys that shaped us

Places, encounters, and roots

Hamsa Nomads grew from real places, real challenges, and real moments of Jewish connection across the world.

India

India became one of our deepest sources of inspiration. From Mumbai to Pune to Kochi, we discovered Jewish stories, communities, and traces of history. In the south, when we thought we would spend Shabbat alone, Hebrew letters on a street led us to a hotel owner, then to a rabbi, and finally to a Shabbat meal we never expected.

Sri Lanka

In Sri Lanka, kashrut became something physical and unforgettable. We went to the ocean to tovel our vessels — the kashering of our kitchen tools in the waves. It was not neat or easy. The water moved, the waves pushed back, and the moment became a reminder that Jewish life while traveling is not always convenient, but it can be deeply alive.

France

France shaped part of the foundation of Hamsa Nomads through years of Jewish community involvement and event planning. Leading and organizing within a Jewish student union taught us how to bring people together, how to create belonging, and how much thoughtful details matter when building a meaningful Jewish experience.

Israel

Israel is where everything is rooted. It is the center of the story, the place where Jewish time, Jewish land, Jewish memory, and Jewish responsibility all become tangible. For Hamsa Nomads, Israel is not just another destination — it is where everything started.

USA

The United States is where everything feels possible. It is where ideas can quickly become real, where communities can be built from scratch, and where Hamsa Nomads can grow into retreats, gatherings, Shabbatonim, and travel experiences for people looking for something warm, beautiful, and Jewish.

Morocco & the Sahara

In Morocco and the Sahara, we felt how Jewish memory can live inside music, names, and stories. Around a narguileh in the desert, an Amazigh man heard a Jewish name and answered with a memory of his own roots. Then came Moroccan Jewish music, darboukah, and the strange feeling of being both far away and deeply connected.

Vietnam

In Vietnam, we were moved by everyday creativity: children inventing games, local ways of cooking, and the challenge of adapting what we saw into our own kosher kitchen. It taught us that travel is not only about seeing places, but about learning how people live.

Japan

In Japan, unexpected conversations opened a window into identity, history, and the ways people relate to Jewish memory from a distance. It reminded us that Jewish presence can spark curiosity and connection in places we never expected.

South Korea

In South Korea, a simple visible sign of Jewish life — like a kippah — became the beginning of conversations. Sometimes the smallest details open the deepest exchanges.

Why we built this

From travel memories to shared experiences

Hamsa Nomads is our way of turning those lived moments into something others can experience too.

What we want to offer

We want to create Jewish travel and retreat experiences that feel beautiful, grounded, and real — not generic programs, but spaces where people can connect, celebrate, rest, and feel part of something warm.

Hamsa Nomads is built from the belief that Shabbat, Chagim, kashrut, hospitality, and Jewish togetherness can travel with us — across countries, landscapes, and moments of life.
