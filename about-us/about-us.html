Our story

Why Hamsa Nomads exists

We created Hamsa Nomads out of lived experience: traveling far, trying to remain faithful to Jewish life, and discovering that some of the most meaningful encounters happen when you least expect them.

We are experienced travelers who spent years moving across Europe and Asia while facing the same question again and again: how do you keep kosher, keep Shabbat and Chag, and remain connected to your faith while crossing cultures, languages, and landscapes?

In India, we spent time with local Jewish communities and discovered remarkable stories from Mumbai to Pune to Kochi. In the south of the country, when we thought we would be spending Shabbat entirely alone, we noticed Hebrew letters on a street. Following that trail led us to a hotel owner, then to the number of a rabbi, and finally to a Shabbat meal we never could have imagined.

That kind of moment stayed with us. It reminded us that Jewish life is not only carried by institutions or cities — it is also carried by memory, kindness, and the invisible threads that connect people across the world.

“We built Hamsa Nomads to share those unforgettable memories — and to create new ones with you.”
Not as spectators, but as people who lived the improvisation, the challenge, the warmth, and the wonder ourselves.
Journeys that shaped us

Places, encounters, and memories

India

India was one of the deepest sources of inspiration for us. From Mumbai to Pune to Kochi, we encountered living Jewish histories and communities. In the south, when we were ready to spend Shabbat alone in the middle of nowhere, a few Hebrew letters on a street led us unexpectedly to a rabbi and a Shabbat meal.

Vietnam

In Vietnam, we were moved by small everyday beauty: children inventing games, local ways of cooking, and the challenge of learning recipes and adapting them into our own kosher kitchen with what we had.

Japan

In Japan, we met people who spoke of a connection to Judaism and history in unexpected ways. Those conversations opened our eyes to how identity and memory can echo across cultures, even where you do not expect them.

Morocco & the Sahara

In Morocco and in the Sahara, we experienced moments of astonishing recognition. Around a narguileh in the desert, an Amazigh man heard the name “Moshe Cohen” and answered, “Are you Jewish? Me too, but centuries ago…” Then came Jewish Moroccan music, voices joining in, and darboukah under the desert sky.

South Korea

In South Korea, even simple curiosity about a kippah became the beginning of conversations about who we are. Sometimes a small visible sign of Jewish life opens a much bigger exchange.

Sri Lanka & France

In Sri Lanka, even toveling vessels became an adventure against the waves. In France, Moshe’s experience leading a Jewish union taught us something essential: people can be brought together around what they share, and Judaism has the power to create belonging, connection, and meaning.

Why we built this

From travel memories to shared experiences

Across all of these journeys, what grew in us was not only resilience, but also a deep desire to gather, to host, and to share.

What we want to offer

Hamsa Nomads is our way of taking those incomparable memories and turning them into something living: thoughtful Jewish travel and retreat experiences built with care, beauty, and meaning.

We want to share the gift of Shabbat, Yom Tov, Chagim, kashrut, hospitality, and Jewish togetherness with people who genuinely want to experience it. That is the spirit behind everything we build.
