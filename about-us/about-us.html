Our story

Jewish life appeared in the most unexpected places.

Again and again, while traveling, we discovered that keeping kosher, keeping Shabbat and Chag, and staying connected to faith was not only a challenge — it was also the source of some of the most unforgettable moments of our lives.

Hamsa Nomads was born from years of travel across Europe and Asia, and from a simple challenge we came to know deeply: how do you keep kosher, keep Shabbat and Chag, and stay rooted in your faith while moving through the world?

Again and again, we found that Jewish life appears in the most unexpected places.

In India, we spent time with local Jewish communities and discovered their remarkable stories from Mumbai to Pune to Kochi. In the south of the country, when we were in the middle of nowhere and preparing to spend Shabbat alone, we suddenly noticed Hebrew letters on a street sign. Following that small clue led us to a hotel owner, then to the phone number of a rabbi, and finally to a Shabbat meal we never could have imagined.

It was one of those unforgettable moments that stays with you forever — and one of the moments that eventually brought Hamsa Nomads into being.

“We built Hamsa Nomads to share those unforgettable memories — and to create new ones with you.”
Not as spectators, but as people who lived the questions, the improvisation, the wonder, and the connection ourselves.
What shaped us

The moments that stayed with us.

These experiences were not just travel memories. They shaped how we think about hospitality, Jewish belonging, and what it means to gather people around something real.

India

From Mumbai to Pune to Kochi, we encountered deep and living Jewish histories. In the far south, a few Hebrew letters on a street led us unexpectedly to a rabbi and a Shabbat meal when we thought we would be alone.

Vietnam, Japan & Asia

In Vietnam, everyday life inspired us — children at play, local cooking, and the challenge of recreating flavors in a kosher way. In Japan and elsewhere in Asia, conversations about Judaism emerged in places we never expected, opening surprising windows into memory, identity, and connection.

Morocco & the Sahara

In Morocco, chance encounters turned into conversations about Jewish identity. In the Sahara, around a narguileh in the desert, we heard someone say, “Are you Jewish? Me too, but centuries ago…” and then share Jewish Moroccan music while others joined in with song and darboukah.

South Korea

Even simple curiosity — a glance at a kippah, a question about who we were — became the beginning of meaningful exchanges about Judaism, visibility, and carrying identity with openness.

Sri Lanka

Even the practical details of Jewish life on the road became part of the story. Toveling vessels while balancing on rocks against the waves reminded us that Jewish life is made real through action, even far from home.

France

In France, Moshe’s experience leading a Jewish union taught us something essential: people can be brought together around what they share, and Judaism has the power to create belonging, meaning, and community.

Our vision

Frum at heart, open in spirit.

Across all of these experiences, we discovered something we wanted to build: a way to share the beauty of Jewish life through travel, hospitality, and gathering.

What Hamsa Nomads stands for

Hamsa Nomads creates kosher, thoughtful, meaningful travel and retreat experiences rooted in Jewish life, designed for people who want to experience the gift of Shabbat, Yom Tov, Chagim, and kashrut in a way that feels alive, elevated, and real.

Our programs are frum at heart, while remaining open and welcoming to those who genuinely want to experience that world with respect, curiosity, and joy.

We built Hamsa Nomads from lived experience — from challenge, inspiration, improvisation, hospitality, and wonder — and we hope every experience we host carries that same feeling forward.
