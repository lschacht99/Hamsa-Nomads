The origin

Rooted, even on the road.

Hamsa Nomads comes from a simple belief: Jewish life does not have to stay inside familiar places to feel deep, beautiful, and real.

What we learned by traveling Jewish

Travel taught us that beauty and challenge often arrive together. You can find breathtaking landscapes, hidden Jewish history, warm strangers, complicated logistics, and moments of deep connection in the same journey.

Keeping kosher, preparing for Shabbat, carrying the right utensils, thinking about timing, food, community, and holiday rhythm — these details could have made travel feel smaller. Instead, they made it more meaningful.

From the road

The Jewish stories most people miss

In India, we encountered Jewish histories that many travelers never hear about — from Mumbai to Kochi, from old synagogues to living communities. It showed us that Jewish life has roots in places people do not always expect.

In Vietnam, the landscapes were unforgettable, but so was the work of adapting: finding ways to cook, plan, and stay connected to our standards while still experiencing the place itself. We did not want travel to mean disconnecting from Jewish life. We wanted both.

Why that became Hamsa Nomads

Over time, we developed a way of traveling that was careful, creative, and alive. We learned how to build Jewish comfort in unfamiliar places — not by making everything generic, but by thinking through the details.

Hamsa Nomads is the next step of that story. We create retreats and gatherings for people who want Jewish life to feel warm, social, beautiful, and a little unexpected — without losing the standards, rhythm, and intention that make it meaningful.

The people behind it

Moshe, Leah, and Ava.

Hamsa Nomads is personal. It comes from the way we travel, host, build, cook, design, and notice the details that make people feel comfortable.

The Hamsa Nomads household Travel, Kosher logistics, creative ideas, and a dog in the middle of everything.
Co-founder

Moshe

Moshe grew up in France and has spent years involved in Jewish community life, organizing events, trips, and gatherings. He previously served as president of a Jewish union, where he learned how much atmosphere, logistics, and people matter when you are building a shared experience.

As an urban planner and lifelong creative, Moshe thinks about how people move through spaces — a table, a room, a city, a retreat weekend — and how the right setting can make strangers feel connected.

Co-founder

Leah

Leah is a mechanical engineer, builder, and deeply creative thinker. She loves turning ideas into real objects, systems, spaces, and experiences — the kind of person who sees possibility everywhere and immediately starts figuring out how to make it work.

She brings the detail, the structure, the design eye, and the constant flow of ideas that make Hamsa Nomads feel more intentional than a standard retreat.

The center of the story — Jewish time, land, memory, responsibility, and the feeling of being connected to something larger.

United States

Where Hamsa Nomads is becoming real: retreats, Shabbatonim, holiday experiences, and new forms of community.

Morocco & the Sahara

Music, memory, names, desert landscapes, and the feeling of being far from home while still finding connection.

Vietnam

Stunning landscapes, local beauty, and the challenge of keeping travel adventurous while staying connected to Jewish practice.

Japan

A place that sharpened the question behind Hamsa Nomads: how do you remain rooted when the surroundings are completely unfamiliar?

South Korea

Another reminder that Jewish identity can open conversations, even in places where you do not expect it.
