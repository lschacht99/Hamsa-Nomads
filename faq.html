Frequently Asked Questions

A few simple answers about how Hamsa Nomads works, what happens after you apply, and what to expect from a curated group retreat.

Hamsa Nomads is for people who want a warm, meaningful, social Jewish travel experience — not just a place to sleep. Each trip is built around a small curated group, shared meals, good conversation, and a real sense of community.
Once you submit the form, we review your application to understand who you are, what kind of experience you’re looking for, and whether the retreat is the right fit. If it looks like a good match, we’ll follow up with next steps, pricing details, and how to reserve your spot.
Your spot is not confirmed by applying alone. A spot is confirmed only after you are accepted and complete the required payment or deposit. Until then, availability may change.
The price generally includes the retreat stay, group meals, Shabbos or holiday programming when relevant, and shared retreat activities. Exact inclusions may vary by trip, so each retreat page or follow-up message will explain what is included for that specific experience.
Transportation is usually not included unless clearly stated for a specific trip. Guests are generally responsible for getting to and from the retreat location. When possible, we may help connect guests who want to coordinate carpools.
Kashrut details depend on the specific retreat. We take kosher food seriously and will share the relevant food setup, supervision details, and kitchen arrangements before payment is finalized. If you have a specific kashrut need, please mention it in your application.
Yes. You can apply with a friend or mention who you would like to come with. Each person may still need to complete their own application so we have everyone’s details, preferences, and emergency or food-related information.
Cancellation policies may vary by retreat because each location has different booking rules and upfront costs. In general, payments may be non-refundable once they are used to secure housing, food, or group arrangements. The exact cancellation policy will be shared before you pay.
Bring comfortable clothing, toiletries, any medications you need, weather-appropriate layers, and anything personal you need for Shabbos, yom tov, or travel. A more specific packing list will be sent before the retreat.
This is a curated group retreat. It is not a hotel program, not a large commercial production, and not a fully anonymous travel package. The goal is a more personal experience: a smaller group, intentional atmosphere, shared meals, and a sense of traveling with people rather than just booking a room.
