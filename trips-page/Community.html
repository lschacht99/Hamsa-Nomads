Jewish Travel Community

The Jewish travel circle we wish already existed.

Hamsa Nomads is building a safe, screened WhatsApp-based community for Jewish travelers who want real travel information, kosher tips, and actual human connection when they are on the road.

Some Israeli and Hebrew-speaking travel networks already exist — but not everyone can access them. If you do not speak Hebrew, do not have an Israeli number, or simply do not know where to start, it can feel like the information is everywhere and nowhere at once.

We want to build the bridge: a place for Jewish travelers from abroad to find the information they need to keep kosher, find community, plan Shabbat, locate synagogues, see who is around, ask practical questions, and not feel alone when they land somewhere new.

Why this exists

Because Jewish travel information should not feel hidden.

When you travel as a Jew, the questions are different. It is not only where to go — it is where to eat, where to spend Shabbat, whether there is a minyan, whether there is a community nearby, and who you can ask when you want real answers.

01

The information is scattered.

Some answers live in private chats. Some live in people’s heads. Some live in Hebrew-only spaces. Some are only discoverable if you already know the right person.

02

Travelers from abroad need their own entry point.

There is a real need for a Jewish travel community that works for English and French speakers first — and grows from there.

03

Safety matters.

This should not be just another open group. We want a space that feels secure, intentional, useful, and built with care for the people inside it.

We want Hamsa Nomads to be the name people trust when they travel.

The goal is simple: wherever Jews go in the world, they should know they can count on Hamsa Nomads to help them find the information, people, and connections they need.

Not a public group.

People leave their information first. Requests are reviewed. We want the space to grow carefully and stay safe.

Not another social feed.

This is not about public exposure, endless noise, or throwing strangers into one giant comment section.

A real travel community.

Jewish travelers should be able to ask real questions: about kosher food, Shabbat, who is around, whether they can create a minyan, and what kind of Jewish life exists locally.

What you’ll find

A practical network for Jewish travelers.

We are building something broader than “just go to Chabad.” Sometimes that may be part of the answer, and sometimes people need more: local community, independent leads, current traveler insight, and human connection.

Kosher travel tips

Restaurants, grocery ideas, food realities on the ground, and the practical details travelers actually want to know.

Shabbat connection

Questions about where to stay, who may be around, how to find a meal, and how to make Shabbat work even far from home.

Local Jewish information

Synagogues, communities, Jewish spaces, and the kind of location-based help that makes travel feel more possible.

Traveler-to-traveler community

A place to ask who is around, who has been there recently, and what the real situation is on the ground.

How it works

A classic structure, built with care.

We want this to grow in a way that stays safe, useful, and grounded. So people are not added blindly — the community is built intentionally.

1

Leave your info

Add your first name, last name, email, WhatsApp number, and optionally where you travel.

2

We review requests

This is not an automatic open invite. We want to know who is coming into the space.

3

We build by language

The first focus is on travelers who do not operate in Hebrew-only networks — especially English and French speakers.

4

We grow by destination

Over time, the network can branch into places and channels based on real traveler needs and real community use.
