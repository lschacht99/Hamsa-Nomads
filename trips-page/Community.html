The line

If you travel somewhere, Hamsa is where you start.

The goal is not only to create a WhatsApp group. The goal is to make Hamsa Nomads the first name people remember when they need Jewish travel information.

Before the trip. During the trip. When plans change. When Shabbat is coming. When you need food, people, a minyan, a host, or a community nearby.

Why it matters

Access should not depend on already knowing the right chat.

  • 01
    Some of the best travel information lives in private Hebrew-speaking networks.
  • 02
    Many Jewish travelers from abroad do not have an Israeli number, Hebrew access, or the right contact.
  • 03
    Public groups are noisy. Public comments can feel exposed. Hamsa should feel safer and more intentional.
  • 04
    Jewish travel is not only logistics. It is food, rhythm, community, prayer, safety, and belonging.
What you can ask

Not only kosher food. The whole Jewish travel layer.

Hamsa Nomads should become the place people check before they go — and the place they come back to when they learn something useful.

Food

Kosher restaurants, groceries, products, delivery, and what actually works locally.

Shabbat

Meals, hosts, where to stay, what areas work, and how to make Shabbat feel possible.

Community

Local Jewish life, people nearby, travelers crossing paths, and who to ask.

Minyan

Host

Where to feel welcomed, where to ask, and how to find real community on the road.
