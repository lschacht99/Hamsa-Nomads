Jewish Travel Community

The circle we wish existed before every trip

Hamsa Nomads is building a safe, screened WhatsApp-based community for Jewish travelers who need kosher travel tips, Shabbat connections, local Jewish information, and real people to ask.

Some Hebrew-speaking travel networks already exist, but they are not always accessible to Jewish travelers from abroad. If you do not speak Hebrew, do not have an Israeli number, or do not know where to start, the information can feel scattered, private, and hard to reach.

We want to build the bridge: a trusted place where Jewish travelers can find what they need to keep kosher, join community, find Shabbat options, locate synagogues, see who else is nearby, and travel with more connection.

Jewish travel information should not feel hidden.

When you travel as a Jew, the questions are different. It is not only where to go. It is where to eat, where to spend Shabbat, whether there is a minyan, what community exists nearby, and who can give you a real answer.

The bigger idea

A Jewish travel network people can count on.

The goal is for Hamsa Nomads to become the name Jewish travelers remember when they land somewhere new and need a place to begin: kosher food, Shabbat, synagogue information, local community, safety context, and real people.

More than addresses

This is not only a list of places to eat. It is a way to understand what Jewish life and Jewish options actually look like in a destination.

More than a trip tip

It is a community where travelers can ask, share, connect, and help each other find their way.

More than one country

We are building this for Jewish travelers moving across cities, borders, languages, and communities.

What the community will help people find.

The first version will be simple: a screened builder list and WhatsApp-based community. As it grows, we can organize by language and destination.

Kosher trip tips

Restaurants, groceries, food realities, products, and practical details travelers want before they arrive.

Shabbat options

Where to stay, who may be around, whether there are meals, and how to make Shabbat work far from home.

Local Jewish info

Synagogues, communities, minyanim, local Jewish spaces, and the kind of location-based help that makes travel easier.

Traveler connection

A way to ask who is nearby, who has been there recently, and what the real situation is on the ground.

How we want to build it.

Slowly, carefully, and with a real sense of responsibility. The community has to stay useful, human, and safe.

1
