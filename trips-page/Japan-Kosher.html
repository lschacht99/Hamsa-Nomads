Suggested route

Compact version: open the parts you need. This keeps the page easier to scan on mobile.

1Start in TokyoChabad, kosher food, Shibuya, Asakusa, museums, neighborhoods

Use Tokyo as your main kosher base and first Shabbat anchor.

  • Shibuya, Harajuku, Meiji Shrine area, Shinjuku, Asakusa, Ueno, Ginza.
  • TeamLab-style digital art, museums, cafés, gardens, skyline viewpoints.
  • Day trips: Nikko, Kamakura, Yokohama, Mount Takao, or Fuji/Hakone.
2Mount Fuji / HakoneViews, lakes, ropeways, onsens, nature break

Build this as a day trip or overnight between Tokyo and Kyoto, depending on weather and Shabbat timing.

  • Lake Kawaguchiko for Fuji views, Chureito Pagoda, cycling, ropeways.
  • Hakone for lake views, cable cars, open-air museum, ryokan atmosphere.
  • Weather matters: do not overbuild the itinerary around Fuji visibility.
3Kyoto Shabbat baseTemples, gardens, Arashiyama, Gion, old streets

Use Kyoto as the second Jewish anchor and plan your hotel location around walking distance.

  • Fushimi Inari, Kiyomizu-dera, Gion, Nishiki Market area, Philosopher’s Path.
  • Arashiyama bamboo grove, river area, gardens, tea culture.
  • Book Shabbat and kosher food ahead; do not assume last-minute availability.
4Osaka + NaraFood-city energy, deer park, temples, day trips

Osaka and Nara are easy from Kyoto, so you can keep one base and avoid too many hotel moves.

  • Osaka: Dotonbori, castle area, Shinsekai, Umeda, aquarium, city energy.
  • Nara: deer park, Todai-ji, Kasuga Taisha, old town walks.
  • Great section for lighter logistics because trains are frequent and easy.
5Hiroshima + MiyajimaPeace history, island shrine, slower ending

A meaningful final extension if you have enough days after Kyoto/Osaka.

  • Hiroshima Peace Memorial Museum and park.
  • Miyajima island, Itsukushima Shrine area, hiking options, coastal views.
  • Plan food carefully because kosher support is more limited away from Tokyo/Kyoto.

Kosher planning notes

  • Contact Tokyo or Kyoto Chabad before arrival for Shabbat meals, holiday meals, kosher food orders, and service details.
  • Do not rely on finding kosher food spontaneously outside the main Jewish bases.
  • For Shabbat, book hotels within walking distance and check key cards, elevators, and route practicality.
  • Bring backup shelf-stable food for train days, mountain stops, and cities without kosher support.

Transportation strategy

Japan is built for trains. Use the Shinkansen for long-distance movement and local trains for city/day trips. Luggage forwarding can make the route easier, especially between Tokyo, Kyoto, and Osaka.

ShinkansenLocal trainsIC cardLuggage forwarding

Best rule: do not arrive in a new city right before Shabbat. Build in time for hotel check-in, food pickup, and walking-route checks.
