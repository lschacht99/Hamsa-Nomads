Shavuos environment

What the weekend is built around.

This page is about the retreat itself: the house, the rhythm, the group, the table, the holiday atmosphere, and the practical pieces that make the weekend work.

Small curated group

Applications help us shape a comfortable, balanced group and coordinate sleeping, meals, rides, programming, and expectations.

Meals and shared tables

Shabbos and Shavuos meals are part of the retreat experience, with food prepared on site and the table treated as one of the main gathering places.

Pool and hot tub timing

The schedule is planned with clear pool and hot tub blocks, including separate men’s and women’s moments so more guests can feel comfortable using the amenities.

Games and learning

Expect a mix of learning, group discussions, relaxed games, Shabbos-friendly activities, and enough unstructured time for real conversations.

Nature and space

Vermont gives the weekend a slower rhythm: fresh air, views, outdoor time, and the feeling of leaving the regular week behind.

Part of Hamsa Nomads

This retreat is one live gathering inside a larger Jewish travel community — a way to meet people now and keep building connection after the weekend.
